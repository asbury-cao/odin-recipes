Pinoy Bistek

Description:

Bistek Tagalog is a classic Filipino dish made of thinly sliced beef braised in a mixture of citrus juice (more commonly, the local fruit, calamansi), soy sauce, onions, garlic, and pepper. A delicious medley of salty, tangy, and savory flavors, itâ€™s traditionally served with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2lbs of sirloin beef
  • Red/White onions (either is fine but I prefer red since it's sweeter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 4 lemons

Steps:

  1. Thaw beef and slice into thin strips
  2. Place in large bowl and add the sugar, salt, soy sauce, and lemons per portion sizes above
  3. Slice onions into thin rings
  4. Pour 2 tablespoons of cooking oil into a large pan
  5. Saute onions until brown then place them into the final serving dish plate
  6. Place beef and sauce mixture into same pan onions were cooked, starting on Low heat
  7. Raise heat up to Medium once all beef is in
  8. Fry beef on both sides until brown and caramelized and sauce is reduced (make sure to uncurl folded slices)
  9. Place only the meat into final serving dish and mix with onions
  10. With remaining sauce in pan, pour 1/2 cups of water in
  11. Allow the sauce to reduce and mix during the process
  12. Pour sauce onto beef and onions

Done! Get some rice on that plate and pour some sauce on top and enjoy!
